About Me – Mindshapes

Hi, I’m Varshini — currently 15 years old.

Writing became more than just a hobby for me after 8th grade, when a prize for my first story sparked something I’d never felt before — a rush of adrenaline, and a will to keep going. Since then, I’ve found my voice in poetry and philosophy. Over the past two months, I’ve written over 25 poems, many of them born from sudden moments of inspiration — pouring thoughts into words when nothing else made sense.

I’ve always enjoyed aptitude-based olympiads, especially the ones introduced to me in 4th grade. I still remember being selected for the second level of the Unified Cyber Olympiad that year — one of my earliest moments of confidence in logical thinking. That curiosity has stayed with me and quietly shaped how I learn.

Reading is something I return to often — whether it's a novel that transports me or a non-fiction book that sharpens my thoughts. I’m also an ardent sports enthusiast, and watching tennis gives me a strange but real surge of motivation — something about the precision, the grit, the solitude.

I have a deep interest in geopolitics and debate, and I’ve participated in multiple Model United Nations and Youth Parliaments, earning awards like High Commendation in the UNSC. These experiences taught me not just how to argue — but how to listen, think deeper, and see multiple sides of a story.

I’ve had a keen interest in Deutsch (German) since 6th grade and have attended several Goethe-Institut workshops. In 9th grade, I earned an A1 level certificate, and I continue to learn German with curiosity and joy. I also enjoy learning new English vocabulary because it’s a language that keeps me constantly engaged — with new words, expressions, and nuances that never fail to interest me.

After 10th grade, I realized how much I genuinely love Physics and Mathematics — not just the formulas, but the logic, the reasoning, the questions behind the answers. That’s when I began preparing for the JEE, shifting my mindset from memorization to understanding.

I’ve also been coding in Python since 6th grade and still explore it in my free time — sometimes for fun, sometimes for structure. Whether it's a game, a puzzle, or just a curiosity, I enjoy the language of code just as much as the rhythm of poetry.

Mindshapes is a space where I try to bring all of this together — my thoughts, my questions, my attempts, and the way I see the world.

It’s not perfect — just real. Like me.
