as i sit near the rippling echoes
i gaze upon the misty clouds
the nature knits a tale
to which i eagerly listen.
i hear just the plink of tears
mother earth sheds upon the world
and see the flowers sparkle with happiness
the roses blossom red, the trees swirl to the rhythm
the god of wind eagerly calls.
the once dry land breathes of bliss,
carrying the scent of renewal.
the birds shriek shrilly;
there is euphoria everywhere just around
yet none of it, in the blue.
the drought comes to an end
and as I behold
to discern that,
it takes just the sacrifice to bloom and suffice.
